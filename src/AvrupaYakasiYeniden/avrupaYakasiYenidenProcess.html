#Planning and Inspiration

The reason for creating this work is my collaboration with Diorama Artist Ahmet Gezer to create a physical copy of this model. I need to replace the previous work with printable models. I'm also taking this opportunity to add a more stylized appearance. I plan to create an animation of the model with more detailed texturing as the camera rotates around it.

My previous work will serve as a reference. Since my previous work was very detailed, I won't have much difficulty with modeling references in this version.

#Modeling

Compared to my previous work, I created much more stylized models with highly detailed texturing. I am very satisfied with the final result.

I am doing the texturing in Substance Painter. This is the most time-consuming part of the project.

#Texturing

I am doing the texturing in Substance Painter. This is the most time-consuming part of the project.

Although I mentioned detailed texturing, I would say it was sufficiently detailed for this scale and camera angle. I didn't spend excessive time on all materials, as it would have taken much longer for a scene at this level of detail.

#Lighting

After several lighting setups, I experimented with physically accurate values, which helped me achieve much more realistic lighting. Initially, I used three large-scale area lights, but I noticed this was making the work appear too flat. When I tried using spot lights at different points in the scene, I achieved much more depth in the image. I'm very satisfied with the final lighting setup.

#Compositing

I placed a channel logo in the top-left corner, similar to those seen in TV series broadcasts.

Applied denoising to enhance image quality.

I added a frame mask, which I believe gave the work a nice retro feel.

Initially added some grain effect but later decided against it and removed it.
