#Planning and Inspiration

I started this project to create a few artworks inspired by the café I love and also work at. Its design has fascinated me since the very first time I visited as a customer. Once I started working there, I thought, “Now’s the perfect time,” and dove into the project. I’m incredibly excited about this journey.

Since the café’s name is Grass, I wanted to emphasize a natural vibe, with grass obviously being the central focus.

The first artwork I want to create is an isometric view showcasing the café and its exterior along the street.

Later, I plan to include close-up shots for a more intimate perspective.

#Modeling

The process of modeling: crafting worlds, battling topology, and occasionally wondering why you started.

Given that the project involves a lot of detail, I avoided obsessing over hyper-specific details in every model. However, I ensured each model had enough detail to stay faithful to reality

I gathered references from photos I took and from the café’s Instagram account.

The modeling process has been ongoing for about a month, and I continue adding new models regularly. I’m quite satisfied with how the models are turning out so far.

#Texturing

I’m handling most of the texturing in Substance Painter, though I texture some smaller objects (like railings or thin poles) directly in Blender.

Due to the detailed nature of this project, I didn’t spend too much time texturing each model individually. Still, I’ve aimed for a sufficient level of detail that balances realism with a cozy, inviting aesthetic.

In some cases, I deviated from real-world references to enhance the environment’s cozy vibe.

#Lighting

Currently, I’m using a sky texture for natural sunlight as the primary light source, paired with a uniform background light for subtle fill lighting. Additionally, I used point lights for the lamps on the upper floor.

For the café’s interior, I stuck with sunlight to stay true to the main focus—the grass and outdoor vibe. Open-air lighting best highlights the grass, which is key to this project.

I aimed for sharp shadows, though not excessively harsh. The lighting leans towards a golden hour palette, with a soft, orange glow for a warm and inviting atmosphere.

#Compositing

For the post-processing stage, I started by adding black bars at the top and bottom of the frame for a cinematic feel.

To give the artwork a retro vibe, I added some grain and played around with color balance, eventually choosing the color palette that felt the most nostalgic to me.

I also incorporated a touch of lens distortion for a subtle, dreamy effect.
